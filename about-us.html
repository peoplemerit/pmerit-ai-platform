The Founder's Journey

Early Struggle

Born as the 14th of 15 children in a polygamous Christian home in Nigeria. Despite his father's wealth as a sawmiller, educational support came with impossible conditions.

  • Walked 3.4km daily from Ogbun Village to Orile Igbore Grammar School
  • Worked on farm and sawmill after school until dark
  • Disowned at age 11 for not meeting demanding expectations
  • Forced to navigate life alone while pursuing education

Turning Point

Through sheer determination and faith, passed WAEC examination. Siblings helped secure college admission, marking the beginning of transformation.

  • Worked countless jobs to fund education
  • Won US visa lottery - a life-changing opportunity
  • Recognized education as the bridge from poverty to possibility

Deep Foundations

In America, built educational credentials while serving the nation, creating a foundation for future impact.

  • BSc in Accounting - University of North Carolina Greensboro
  • Master's in Accounting & Finance - Purdue University
  • 4 years service in US National Guard
  • Witnessed similar struggles in underserved American communities

Vision & Growth

Realized millions of brilliant minds worldwide are trapped not by lack of potential, but by lack of accessible education. Envisioned AI as the breakthrough solution.

  • Identified universal educational barriers across Nigeria and US
  • Developed culturally intelligent approach to learning
  • Combined Nigerian lived experience with American innovation
  • Designed dual-market strategy for global impact

Execution

Launched PMERIT to serve 3+ billion underserved learners worldwide. One child's fight for education became a global mission.

  • Built 21 operational containers with enterprise-grade infrastructure
  • Deployed Gabriel AI for personalized tutoring
  • Established partnerships with TD Africa, Cloudflare, Andela
  • Created platform ready to break poverty cycles globally

Global Vision & Regional Focus

Africa

South Asia

1.8 billion learners. Addressing educational gaps through AI-powered personalization, multilingual support, and culturally adapted curricula.

Latin America

600+ million learners. Bridging digital divide with accessible technology, remote work skills, and economic empowerment programs.

Rural Communities

Global rural populations. Delivering education where infrastructure is limited through offline-first design and low-bandwidth solutions.

Three-Tier Career Architecture

General Research & Content (GRC)

Foundation layer providing comprehensive curriculum development, research-backed learning materials, and culturally adaptive content for all regions.

Learning Curricula Platform (LCP)

Core platform delivering structured learning paths, AI-powered assessments, career guidance, and progress tracking across all domains.

Learning Experience & Delivery (LEd)

User-facing layer with Gabriel AI tutoring, virtual classrooms, community forums, and personalized learning experiences that adapt to each learner.
