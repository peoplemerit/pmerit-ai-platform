CONSTITUTION OF PMERIT
________________________________________

PREAMBLE

We, the trustees of PMERIT (People Merit), firmly committed to breaking poverty cycles through accessible, AI-powered education that empowers individuals with practical skills and knowledge to improve their lives, do hereby enact and adopt this constitution as the guiding document for the governance of our organization.

This constitution governs the overall PMERIT mission and its organizational structure, which includes:
- **PMERIT Holdings LLC** (Parent Entity - Maine)
- **PMERIT Foundation** (501(c)(3) Nonprofit Subsidiary)
- **PMERIT Technologies LLC** (For-Profit Subsidiary)

________________________________________

ARTICLE 1: NAME AND IDENTITY

The name of the organization shall be PMERIT, which stands for "People Merit."

**Tagline:** "Knowledge as Liberation"

**Guiding Scripture:** "My people perish for lack of knowledge" - Hosea 4:6

The organizational structure shall operate as:
1. **PMERIT Holdings LLC** - Parent holding company (Maine LLC)
2. **PMERIT Foundation** - 501(c)(3) nonprofit for educational mission
3. **PMERIT Technologies LLC** - For-profit technology operations

________________________________________

ARTICLE 2: REGISTERED OFFICES

**Nigeria Operations:**
The registered office for Nigerian operations shall be located at Port Harcourt, Nigeria.

**United States Operations:**
The registered office for U.S. operations shall be located in the State of Maine, United States.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 3: MISSION STATEMENT

**Primary Mission:**
Empowering underserved communities—with primary focus on the USA and Nigeria/Africa—through accessible education and remote career opportunities. The platform prioritizes free-tier access and low-bandwidth optimization to reach 3+ billion users globally over a 15-year roadmap.

**Core Belief:**
We believe that education breaks poverty cycles. By learning new skills, everyone can create better opportunities for themselves, support their families, and build a brighter future. When one person succeeds, we all benefit—"I am because we are" (Ubuntu Principle).

This mission shall be pursued through:
- **PMERIT Foundation:** Free educational content, grant-funded programs, community workshops, and AI-tutored learning for underserved populations
- **PMERIT Technologies LLC:** Platform development, AI infrastructure, premium features, and sustainable revenue generation

________________________________________

ARTICLE 4: OBJECTIVES

The objectives of PMERIT are:

1. To provide free, AI-powered training and skill-building programs for individuals to improve their livelihoods.
2. To create accessible educational content optimized for low-bandwidth and mobile-first delivery across underserved regions.
3. To support community-driven projects aimed at self-reliance and sustainable development.
4. To foster knowledge-sharing and collaboration within communities to promote growth and development.
5. To develop and maintain technology platforms that deliver educational content at scale to 3+ billion potential learners.
6. To seek and secure funding through grants, donations, investments, and revenue generation to sustain the mission.
7. To provide culturally intelligent education that respects and adapts to local contexts while delivering globally recognized credentials.
8. To bridge the digital divide through offline-first design and low-bandwidth solutions for rural communities.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 5: TARGET POPULATIONS AND GEOGRAPHIC SCOPE

**5.1 Primary Markets**
- **United States:** Underserved communities, rural populations, workforce development
- **Nigeria/Africa:** 1.3 billion potential learners across the continent

**5.2 Secondary Markets**
- **South Asia:** 1.8 billion potential learners
- **Latin America:** 600+ million potential learners
- **Rural Communities Worldwide:** Populations with limited infrastructure

**5.3 Global Reach**
Target: 3+ billion underserved learners over a 15-year roadmap.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 6: ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE

**6.1 PMERIT Holdings LLC (Parent Entity)**
- 100% founder-owned Maine LLC
- Holds ownership of all subsidiary entities
- Provides strategic oversight and governance
- Maintains mission alignment across all entities

**6.2 PMERIT Foundation (501(c)(3) Nonprofit)**
- Tax-exempt educational organization
- Delivers free educational programs and content
- Receives grants and tax-deductible donations
- Governed by a Board of Directors (minimum 3 members)
- Subject to IRS 501(c)(3) requirements

**6.3 PMERIT Technologies LLC (For-Profit)**
- Single-member LLC owned by PMERIT Holdings
- Develops and maintains AI-powered technology platform
- Generates revenue through premium services
- Eligible for SBIR/STTR grants and venture investment
- Employs staff and holds intellectual property

________________________________________

ARTICLE 7: EDUCATIONAL MODEL

**7.1 Three-Track Educational Architecture**

The PMERIT platform delivers education through three distinct tracks:

1. **Track 1: Global Remote**
   - Adults seeking remote-work-ready skills
   - Career pathways for global employment
   - AI Tutor: Professor Ada (professional mentor)

2. **Track 2: Local Education (K-12)**
   - Children and students in formal education
   - Grade spans aligned with educational standards (K-5, 6-8, 9-Diploma)
   - Curriculum from curated open-source content (MOOSE, freeCodeCamp, etc.)
   - Parent portal required for minors

3. **Track 3: Local Career (CTE)**
   - Career and technical education
   - Employment-focused skills training
   - Industry-recognized credentials

**7.2 Core Principles**
- **Universal Access:** Education as a human right, not a privilege
- **Ubuntu Principle:** "I am because we are" - community success through individual empowerment
- **Technology for Good:** AI empowers, not replaces human potential
- **Cultural Intelligence:** Respect and adaptation to local cultures
- **Ethical Framework:** Transparency, privacy, and fairness in all operations

**7.3 Credential System**
- 5-level credential hierarchy with blockchain verification (Polygon)
- Verifiable, tamper-proof achievements
- Career mapping to credentials

________________________________________

ARTICLE 8: MEMBERSHIP

1. Membership in PMERIT shall be open to individuals committed to the organization's mission and objectives.
2. Membership categories and terms shall be determined by the trustees and outlined in the bylaws.
3. Foundation membership (for 501(c)(3) purposes) shall be governed by Foundation bylaws.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 9: TRUSTEES AND GOVERNANCE

**9.1 PMERIT Holdings LLC**
- Managed by the Founder as sole member
- Strategic decisions require founder approval
- May appoint advisory board as needed

**9.2 PMERIT Foundation Board of Directors**
- Minimum of three (3) directors required
- Directors must be individuals of sound mind and good character
- Founder may serve as a director
- Directors shall serve terms as defined in Foundation bylaws
- Board oversees Foundation operations, grant compliance, and mission alignment

**9.3 PMERIT Technologies LLC**
- Managed by PMERIT Holdings LLC as sole member
- Day-to-day operations managed by appointed officers
- Strategic decisions require Holdings approval

**9.4 Powers and Duties of Trustees/Directors:**
- Oversee the governance and strategic direction of respective entities
- Ensure compliance with legal and financial obligations
- Appoint officers to manage day-to-day operations
- Ensure mission alignment across all activities

________________________________________

ARTICLE 10: BOARD OF OFFICERS

Each entity may appoint officers, including but not limited to:

1. **Chairperson/President:** To preside over meetings and represent the organization in official capacities.
2. **Secretary:** To maintain accurate records of meetings and correspondence.
3. **Treasurer/CFO:** To oversee financial matters and ensure accurate record-keeping.
4. **Executive Director:** To manage day-to-day operations (Foundation).
5. **Chief Technology Officer:** To oversee platform development (Technologies LLC).

________________________________________

ARTICLE 11: MEETINGS

1. Foundation Board of Directors shall meet at least quarterly.
2. A quorum for Foundation board meetings shall consist of a majority of directors.
3. Decisions shall be made by a simple majority vote.
4. Holdings LLC decisions made by sole member as needed.
5. Annual meetings shall be held to review organizational progress and finances.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 12: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

**12.1 General Principles**
1. All funds shall be used solely for the objectives of the organization.
2. Financial records shall be maintained and audited annually.
3. No part of the organization's income shall benefit any trustee, director, or officer inappropriately.

**12.2 PMERIT Foundation Finances**
- Funds derived from grants, donations, and program revenue
- Tax-exempt status maintained per IRS requirements
- Annual Form 990-N (under $50K) or 990-EZ ($50K-$200K) filing required
- No private benefit or inurement permitted

**12.3 PMERIT Technologies LLC Finances**
- Revenue from platform services, contracts, and investments
- Pass-through taxation to Holdings LLC
- Standard business accounting practices
- May distribute profits to Holdings LLC

**12.4 PMERIT Holdings LLC Finances**
- Receives distributions from subsidiaries
- Manages inter-entity financial relationships
- Pass-through taxation to founder

________________________________________

ARTICLE 13: MISSION PROTECTION

1. The PMERIT Foundation's 501(c)(3) status provides legal mission lock.
2. Foundation assets must be used exclusively for educational and charitable purposes.
3. Upon dissolution of Foundation, assets transfer to similar 501(c)(3) organizations.
4. Technologies LLC operations must support overall PMERIT mission.
5. Holdings LLC Operating Agreement shall include mission protection clauses.
6. Free-tier educational access shall be maintained as a core commitment.
7. The platform shall remain optimized for low-bandwidth and underserved populations.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 14: AMENDMENTS

This constitution may be amended by:
1. Unanimous consent of Holdings LLC member (for Holdings matters)
2. Two-thirds majority vote of Foundation Board of Directors (for Foundation matters)
3. Holdings LLC member approval (for Technologies LLC matters)

Provided 14 days' prior notice is given for Foundation board votes.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 15: DISSOLUTION

**15.1 PMERIT Foundation Dissolution**
Upon dissolution, remaining Foundation assets shall be distributed to organizations exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code with similar educational missions serving underserved populations.

**15.2 PMERIT Technologies LLC Dissolution**
Upon dissolution, remaining assets transfer to PMERIT Holdings LLC or as directed by Holdings.

**15.3 PMERIT Holdings LLC Dissolution**
Upon dissolution, remaining assets shall be:
1. First, used to satisfy any outstanding obligations
2. Second, transferred to PMERIT Foundation (if still operating)
3. Third, distributed to organizations with similar educational missions serving underserved populations

________________________________________

ARTICLE 16: INTERPRETATION

Any questions regarding the interpretation of this constitution shall be resolved by the appropriate governing body, whose decision shall be final.

________________________________________

ARTICLE 17: EFFECTIVE DATE AND TRANSITION

This constitution supersedes previous versions and reflects the Mission Holding Company structure adopted December 2025.

The transition from L3C to Mission Holding Company structure shall be completed per the timeline established in the PMERIT Mission Holding Company Formation Guide.
