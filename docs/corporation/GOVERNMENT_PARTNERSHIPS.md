## PART 1: GOVERNMENT PARTNERSHIP LANDSCAPE

### 1.1 Why Government Partners with Organizations Like PMERIT

1. **Workforce Development Crisis**
   - Skills gap costs US economy $1.2 trillion annually
   - 75% of employers report difficulty finding skilled workers
   - Government actively seeks innovative training solutions

2. **Digital Divide Concerns**
   - Rural areas lack quality educational technology
   - PMERIT's offline-first design addresses this gap
   - Federal priority: broadband alternatives for underserved areas

3. **AI Education Initiative**
   - Biden Administration's AI education priorities
   - States need AI-powered learning platforms
   - PMERIT positioned as AI-native educational solution

4. **Cost Efficiency**
   - Traditional education: $15,000-$50,000+ per student annually
   - PMERIT model: Near-zero marginal cost per student
   - Government seeks scalable, cost-effective solutions

### 2.3 SBIR/STTR Programs (Technologies LLC)

#### Overview

**SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research)**
- For small businesses (<500 employees)
- No nonprofit involvement required
- Three phases: I ($50-250K), II ($750K-1.5M), III (unlimited)

**STTR (Small Business Technology Transfer)**
- Requires research institution partner
- Otherwise similar to SBIR
- Good for university collaborations

#### Relevant Agencies

| Agency | Budget | Focus Areas | PMERIT Fit |
|--------|--------|-------------|------------|
| **Department of Education** | $6M/year | EdTech, Assessment | HIGH |
| **NSF** | $200M/year | AI, Learning Tech | HIGH |
| **Department of Labor** | $10M/year | Workforce Tech | HIGH |
| **Department of Defense** | $1.5B/year | Training Simulations | MEDIUM |
| **NIH** | $1.1B/year | Health Education | LOW-MEDIUM |

#### SBIR Phase I Application Strategy

**Timeline:**
1. Register on SAM.gov (2-4 weeks)
2. Create account on agency portal
3. Find open solicitations (sbir.gov)
4. Submit proposal (typically 15-25 pages)
5. Review period: 3-6 months
6. Award notification

**Key Success Factors:**
- Clear innovation narrative
- Strong technical team
- Commercialization potential
- Prior research/pilot data

**PMERIT Competitive Advantages:**
- Functional platform (not just concept)
- AI-native architecture (timely topic)
- Global scalability demonstrated
- Dual-entity structure (Foundation for research, Tech for commercialization)

### 2.4 Department of Labor (DOL)

#### Workforce Innovation & Opportunity Act (WIOA)

**What It Is:** Primary federal workforce development legislation

**Funding Streams:**
- Title I: Adult, Dislocated Worker, Youth programs
- Title II: Adult Education (flows through states)
- Title III: Wagner-Peyser Employment Services
- Title IV: Vocational Rehabilitation

**PMERIT Alignment:**
- Track 1 (Global Remote) = Adult workforce training
- Track 3 (Local Career) = Pre-apprenticeship preparation
- AI tutoring = innovative delivery mechanism

**Access Strategy:**
1. Partner with local Workforce Development Boards
2. Become approved training provider in state ETPL (Eligible Training Provider List)
3. Offer Individual Training Account (ITA) eligible programs

#### Apprenticeship Expansion

**DOL Registered Apprenticeship:**
- PMERIT can't BE an apprenticeship (requires employer)
- PMERIT CAN be pre-apprenticeship provider
- Partner with employers to create pathways

**Opportunity:**
- Register as pre-apprenticeship program
- Connect completers to employer apprenticeships
- Access DOL apprenticeship grants through partners

## PART 4: CONTRACT OPPORTUNITIES

### 4.1 SAM.gov Registration (Critical First Step)

**What You Need:**
1. EIN for Technologies LLC
2. DUNS Number (now UEI - Unique Entity Identifier)
3. Business bank account
4. Valid email domain (not gmail)

**Registration Process:**
1. Request UEI at SAM.gov (2-3 business days)
2. Complete SAM.gov registration (1-2 weeks)
3. Activate annually (free, but mandatory)

**Important:**
- Register Technologies LLC (not Foundation) for contracts
- Foundation can be sub-recipient on grants
- Both can be registered, but serve different purposes

### 4.2 Contract Vehicles

**GSA Schedule (Long-term Goal)**
- Pre-negotiated contract with GSA
- Agencies can order directly
- Takes 6-12 months to obtain
- Revenue threshold typically required

**Open Market Contracts**
- Available immediately after SAM registration
- Smaller contracts (<$250K simplified acquisition)
- Good for building past performance

**Set-Aside Programs**

| Program | PMERIT Eligibility | Advantage |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|
| Small Business | Yes (Technologies LLC) | Preference in bidding |
| 8(a) (disadvantaged) | Potentially | Major set-asides |
| HUBZone | If located in HUBZone | Sole-source eligible |
| SDVOSB (Veteran) | If founder qualifies | Sole-source eligible |
| WOSB (Women-owned) | No | - |

**Founder Background Check:**
- 4 years US National Guard service
- May qualify for veteran-owned programs
- Research SDVOSB requirements

### 4.3 Finding Contract Opportunities

**SAM.gov Contract Opportunities**
- All federal contracts over $25K posted
- Set filters for NAICS codes:
  - 611420: Computer Training
  - 611430: Professional Development Training
  - 541511: Custom Computer Programming
  - 541519: Other Computer Related Services

**Agency-Specific Portals**
- Department of Education: ed.gov/fund/grants-apply.html
- NSF: nsf.gov/funding/
- DOL: dol.gov/agencies/eta/grants

1. Mission-aligned structure (Foundation for grants, Technologies for contracts)
