## ARTICLE 2: PURPOSE

**2.1 Purpose.** The Company is formed for the following purposes:

(a) To develop, maintain, and operate technology platforms for educational purposes;

(b) To provide premium software services, including but not limited to AI tutoring, text-to-speech, and credential verification;

(c) To hold and manage intellectual property related to the PMERIT educational platform;

(d) To pursue government contracts, SBIR/STTR grants, and commercial opportunities;

(e) To employ staff for the development and operation of educational technology;

(f) To engage in any lawful business activity permitted under Maine law.

**2.2 Mission Alignment.** The Company's activities shall support the overall PMERIT mission of providing accessible education to underserved communities. The Company may generate revenue and profit, which may be distributed to the Member or reinvested in the business.

## ARTICLE 10: DISSOLUTION

**10.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall dissolve upon:

(a) Written consent of the Member;

(b) Entry of a judicial decree of dissolution;

(c) Any other event required by law.

**10.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up by the Member (or appointed liquidator). Company assets shall be applied in the following order:

(a) Payment of debts and obligations;

(b) Payment of liquidation expenses;

(c) Distribution of remaining assets to the Member.
